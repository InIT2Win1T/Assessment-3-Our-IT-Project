Group Reflection

 

Simon

 

I am once again highly content with how our group has worked for Assignment 3. We have taken on board some comments from the assessment of Assignment 2 and applied these accordingly. Once again, we have delegated tasks between us and shared a workload, with all of us contributing to the creation of tangible artifacts to include within this assignment. Everyone has made worthy contribution and pulled their weight. There was no conflict in terms of task selection and delegation.

 

Although Microsoft Teams was good for storing files for everyone to use it was at times prone to lag. Other platforms may have helped us get around this.

 

Every member of the team has been dedicated, despite us all dealing with lockdowns, busy lives, and unexpected circumstances. It was surprising that there was little to no hindrance towards our work despite some of these situations. We could reschedule a meeting at times, or if one member was absent, they were able to review the recorded meeting and catch up easily.

 

I have not learned much more about groups than what I already did during Assignment 2 but one thing worth mentioning is how we can improve if we reflect on feedback as a group, rather than everyone going away and just thinking about it individually.

 

 

Justin

 

Reflecting on Assignment 3 it was a bit easier than Assignment 2 and after working together for this period it is almost habit to prepare for our meetings on Wednesdays and Thursdays. Again, Nickolas showed strong leadership skills in organising the group and running the meetings which was the foundation of us working so well as a team. It definitely helped that this time around we did not have to deal with a member leaving part way through the assignment. There were no conflicts and no hiccups in our work this time around. We allocated the jobs early and everyone completed their work when they were supposed to which was great. Simon deserves a shoutout for putting the assignment together and also identifying that we had missed a section when allocating the work and he jumped straight in and completed that section himself. Meetings could still be a little bit quiet but I think this was because we all had a good idea of what we needed to do and where we were at as a group and individually. Meeting twice a week was probably too much this time around but it did allow us to keep in touch which was nice. In summary this has been a great group to work with and I am proud of the work we have done and the way we went about it.

 

 

Shang

 

The assigned roles based on previous personality traits have worked wonderfully. Under terrific leadership, tasks for Assignment 3 and 5 were allocated based on free will and approved upon consensus. Throughout the weeks, every member of the team was made aware of how each other is progressing in their respective task at the start of every session and discussion around issues and concerns were carried out either on Microsoft Teams or on Discord. Clear direction from Nickolas, collaboration between passionate group members and open channels for communication have facilitated the delivery of this assignment.

 

Our group did encounter some hiccups here and there. As this is a group assignment and everyone has his/her life agenda, Inability to attend a scheduled meeting is somewhat to be expected. We resolved this simply by postponing meeting to another day. We also had to contend with internet connection issues on MS Meet at times but that did not disrupt the final deliverables.

 

Overall, I am satisfied with the groupwork we have submitted this semester and am appreciative of everyone that persevered in the team.

 

 

Josh

 

The group came out of Assignment 2 feeling really good about how we had done and we were eager to start A3. We were once again very organised and I think that organisation from Simon and Nick really helped the group get a great start on this assignment, which was definitely one of our biggest strengths. What could be improved though was the systems and methods we used to communicate as midway through the assignment Shang was unable to connect to Microsoft Teams for over a week, which posed an issue for us as we were unable to verbally communicate with him for a week, though we were still in constant contact with him through discord. I was surprised, as was everyone with how low our A2 marks were as we really felt like we did an amazing job, but although everyone was disappointed it gave us a lot of motivation to improve and better ourselves for the rest of the assignment. What my main take away from this group and assignment would be is how important your communication tools are, and how one minor hiccup can make such a huge effect on how the group functions.

 

 

Nickolas

 

Looking at Assignment 3 comparative to Assignment 2, I believe that this time around we were a lot more prepared and aware of what was required. As a group I believe that we worked very well together, better then Assignment 2 if that is possible. Every member actively assisted the others with every part of the assignment and participated very well during all meetings. As we had a much more in depth understanding of what was required from us as a group, perhaps having 2 weekly meetings might have been more then what was required, but it was not a negative thing. As with Assignment 2, Simon worked incredibly hard and assisted everyone whenever it was needed. Overall, as a team, I am extremely happy with the work everyone put in, both as individuals and as a collective. I would happily work again with this group given the chance.

 

 

Collective Group Reflection

 

As with Assignment 2, there is a strong consensus amongst group members that as a group, we performed well as a group as well as on our individual assigned tasks. Every group member agrees that each member was well-organized, that every group member pulled their own weight, and that we have achieved work of a considerable standard.

 

There is a general consensus that Microsoft Teams had some shortcomings in that it would lag and because Shang was unable to use it for a week. Despite the occasional challenge with tools, we managed to pull through well. One advantage we had this time, is that we didn’t have to deal with a team member part-way through as we did while working on Assignment 2.

 

The delegation of tasks for this assignment was effective in that we all selected roles that were appropriate given our skill set and abilities. There was no conflict regarding this or any other matters.

 

Our marks for Assignment 2, while not entirely bad, were still lower than what we were hoping for and for how well we thought we had went. We did however, take some motivation from this to work harder and take on board any feedback.

 

Members of this group have at times had unforeseen and difficult circumstances in their lives which occasionally meant members were unable to attend meetings. Usually, we would just postpone the meeting but in other instances where we did not, the absent member would watch the recording of the missed meeting and still be on top of everything that the group was doing. There was little to no hindrance therefore, to our work despite occasional difficulties such as these.

 

As a group, everyone feels the work is of a high standard and we feel the group dynamics have enabled this- each individual member has a unique set of skills and strengths- Nick’s leadership and web development skills, Justin’s analytical mindset, Josh and Shang’s abilities to dive into research and apply the information to our project in a relevant way, and Simon’s willingness to help.

 

Overall, we are all collectively content with the work we have produced and would be content to work together in future should the opportunity to do so arise.
