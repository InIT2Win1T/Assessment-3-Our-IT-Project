Scope and Limitations

 

Our project is a tool to assist AFL Fantasy users by giving them information in forms such as boxplots and line graphs in order to give them more specific information. We intended for this to be developed as both a website and as an app for Android and iOS, In the early stages of developing our project, it would be more feasible to develop the website first, and then leave the app as a long-term goal provided the website is successful early on. This is because app development would be a more time-consuming task and we will need to develop the app specifically for both Apple and Android. (Stevens, 2021).

 

Websites, according to Stevens (2021) are less expensive to build and maintain and they can run across numerous platforms. Apps do however run faster and potentially offer more functionality but due to the relative simplicity of our project- being a tool for statistics, a website would be sufficient. Focussing on a website would keep our scope minimal at the beginning and would still be able to be accessed by a range of users on different platforms via their web browsers.

 

We have chosen a 15-month time frame to complete the project. A 6-week time on this assignment for a "whiteboarding" phase to plan the various elements of our project, and then another hypothetical 10 weeks to begin working on defining, designing and developing our hypothetical project.

 

After completing the “whiteboarding” phase in the first few weeks, we would begin creating a map for the website, undertaking some market research, beginning creating wireframes for the aesthetics of the site, and creating a web scraping system. Our web scraping system would consist of using Apify to scrape information of AFL players from relevant websites and Excel will store the information and will be used to generate graphs for users of the website.

 

Another priority for these 10 weeks is to acquire the hosting we need- that is the virtual machine with Amazon LightSail as well as be using cPanel to host and manage the various development services. This will enable us to begin creating the back-end code for the website, which could be begun during these 10 weeks. We would then begin creating mock-ups for elements of our website design and user interface, particularly the player cards. One feature of the player cards. Some elements of the player cards, such as trend analysis may require artificial intelligence programming that is likely to be developed and updated into the player cards during later stages.
