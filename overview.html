Overview

 

Our project is to design a web and app-based tool with a basic free version and paid premium version that will allow users that take part in AFL Fantasy to easily make informed decisions regarding their teams using statistical analysis that is presented in an easy to read and easy to use format. By collecting data and analysing it within Microsoft Excel we will aim to provide our users with a graphical interface that looks at a range of statistics for each AFL player and their AFL Fantasy scores such as high and low scores, standard deviation, scoring bands and their career, season and recent averages. We will add another layer to this by enabling our users to easily add variables to further their insight into a player. For example, our users can add a filter so that they can look at the scores a player has achieved against a certain opponent or at a particular venue. Effective use of this tool can help our users make better informed decisions when they are picking their team and making trades throughout the year.

 

The main feature of our tool will be player cards which is where the user can look at and filter all of this information. Every player in the AFL will have their own card which contains all of their Fantasy scores for all time, trend analysis commentary, upcoming opponents, scoring bands, consistency rating and form which is the average of their last 3 games.  Next to the upcoming opponents there will also be the players form average against that opponent. We will also have some additional features which are more related to people’s teams such as a team builder which allows players to create and save different team combinations without editing their actual team, a section which details players that play either very strongly or very poorly against particular teams and a bot which can suggest players that are going to have good or bad runs of form based on their scoring history.

 

This project has come about as there is nothing that allows someone to easily access this level of statistical analysis and there is nothing that allows someone to select a player and look at not just their scores but add the filters discussed above to use trends in scoring to advantage. As a passionate AFL fan and a Fantasy AFL player I am excited by the idea that I could tap a few buttons and see that the player I am looking to trade in actually has very poor form against his next 3 opponents and that I should look for an alternative. It will save hours of looking on other websites or even creating my own spreadsheets to make decisions.

 

Our closest competitor would be the Fantasy Coach add on that can be purchased for $20 per year. This will show projected scores and breakevens (The minimum a player needs to score to retain their current value). This is the only tool that allows independent research and it is very limited in what it can produce and has no visual element. Other competitors are the various podcasts that analyse fantasy football scores such as the Traders on AFL.com.au or the Lane Kicking podcast. These podcasts give their opinions on players that they have crunched the numbers on. Our service will allow you to select a player and have all of the numbers crunched for you. There are also websites such as Footywire that have scores that can be analysed by the user but these are neither user friendly or built for the purpose of decision making like our tool. None of these competitors have visual outputs, consistency ratings, filtering options or (podcasts as the exception) recommendations on who to pick.

 

While our tool will not bring something brand new to the world of IT our tool can provide a relevant, informative and easy to use comparison which will give our users a competitive advantage on their opponents while also simplifying and reducing time spent on their research.
